01

BAM vs VCF — reads vs. conclusions

The single most important distinction for understanding this project's data. Every archaic individual can exist as either (or both), and they are not interchangeable.

BAM
Binary Alignment Map
The actual sequencing reads — every short DNA fragment, placed ("aligned") on the reference genome, carrying per-base quality, mapping quality, and the ancient-DNA damage still visible at read ends. Large (GBs). From a BAM you can re-derive coverage, assess damage, and (re-)call genotypes any way you like. The raw-ish evidence.
VCF
Variant Call Format
The genotype conclusions drawn from a BAM: at chosen positions, "this individual is A/G", plus depth/quality annotations. Small (MBs). You cannot recover the underlying reads or the damage signal from a VCF — the read-level information is gone. A derived summary.

The analogy

A BAM is all the raw crime-scene photographs from every angle — you can re-measure anything later. A VCF is the detective's written findings ("at spot X the allele is G"). Once you keep only the VCF, you can't go back and re-examine the photos. This is exactly why mapDamage, a max-depth filter, and any transversion/transition handling need BAMs — they read the photographs; a VCF has already thrown them away.

02

The raw public data

The archaic genomes are published — as BAMs aligned to hg19/GRCh37 — in two public archives: MPI-EVA (Max Planck, ftp.eva.mpg.de) and EBI-ENA (ftp.sra.ebi.ac.uk). Lily's pipeline started from these BAMs and called its own genotypes. Sources marked "confirm" are being recovered in Stage 0 of the rebuild.

BAMs only — no FASTQ (confirmed from thesis §2.3)

The archaic data was taken as BAM files "previously mapped to the GRCh37/hg19 reference" — i.e. the reads were already aligned by the original data producers; Lily did no alignment, adapter-trimming, or read-collapsing herself, and there was no FASTQ. The modern SGDP data came as VCFs (also hg19). Consequence for the rebuild: re-mapping to hg38 would require going back to FASTQ and re-running the whole aDNA read-processing chain (and the MPI-EVA genomes are published only as processed BAMs) — so rebuilding on hg19 is the practical choice, not just the cheap one.

Coverage — exact, current

Per-sample mean depth over the panel, computed exactly from the committed *_depth.txt (full record + caveats: coverage_current.tsv). Read these as caveated: they're measured at the panel's current (hg38) mis-located coordinates (bug A2), so they are realized depths at the wrong positions, not true per-locus coverage; Denisova 3's 0.5× is artifactual (bug A1 — its true coverage is ~30×); and a max far above the mean (Vindija 87, Scladina) is a pileup artifact that motivates the max-depth cap. True per-locus coverage comes after the rebuild (Q6). 10 of the 14 samples are under ~3.2× — which is why hard-calling is the wrong tool and we move to genotype likelihoods (ANGSD).

What "contig" means (it comes up a lot): a contig ("contiguous sequence") is one continuous stretch of the reference the reads align to — in a human reference, essentially each chromosome, plus small unplaced/decoy pieces. Every BAM and VCF opens with a header line per contig, @SQ SN:<name> LN:<length>, and the SN value is its label. "Contig naming" is whether that label is bare (1) or prefixed (chr1). Two files can describe the same genome yet be unusable together if one says 1 and the other chr1 — the software matches by name, finds nothing, and silently returns empty. That one-sample mismatch is bug A1 (Denisova 3's BAM was chr-named against a bare-named reference and panel), which is why the "rename contigs" step above harmonizes every sample to bare names up front.

05

Are the samples all the same? No.

They differ on nearly every axis that matters — which is why one uniform recipe never fit them.

Format / source
single BAMs (ENA accessions) vs per-chromosome BAMs (Vindija, MPI-EVA); different filenames and conventions.
Coverage
~4 high-coverage (~16–30×: Vindija 33.19, Chagyrskaya 8, Denisova 5, Denisova 25) vs the rest very low (0.1–3×). This drives the missingness story.
Lab treatment
some UDG-treated (damage largely removed), some not — changes how much read-end deamination there is, and how aggressively we must handle it.
Type
Neanderthal, Denisovan, and the Denisova 11 F1 hybrid. Note Denisova 5 is a Neanderthal despite the cave name.
Contig naming
Denisova 3's BAM was chr-prefixed while the others were bare — bug A1.
Data at all
El Sidrón (Sid1253) has none → 14 analyzed, not 15.
Current cluster state
the full sorted BAMs are gone (only wrong-region filtered_*.bam + already-called VCFs remain) — which is why the rebuild re-downloads the raw reads.
